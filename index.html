Canoe Rentals

If you are a non-member, you have the option to rent a canoe for:
$35 for the day
$25 for a half day (after 3 p.m. or under 3 hours)
$15.00 for one hour

This price includes a tin canoe, paddles and lifejackets

Equipment: Our canoes are aluminum with 2 seats. You may take a 3rd person sitting in the middle-bottom of the canoe.
To prevent capsizing, ensure proper balance in the boat by distributing weight evenly.

Safety Notes: Yes - Canoes do tip over. If you are not prepared for this to happen you should not go out. Windy conditions are also a factor, making it easier to tip.

All paddlers must be confident they can swim if the canoe does tip over.

  • Stay close to shore
  • Stay close to the Clubhouse in poor weather conditions
  • Please stay off the race course
  • Give right of way to all other vessels on the reservoir (SS Moyie paddleboat, Rowers, Dragon Boats and competitive canoes and kayaks)
  • Clothing suggestions: We suggest you wear clothing that is not restrictive in case you do get wet.

Please note: Rentals are first come first serve and no reservations are taken. With over 25 canoes we typically have plenty of boats. You can call ahead for those very warm, sunny weekend afternoons to check on availability if wanted. Reservoir bylaw also requires lifejackets to be worn at all times while on the Glenmore reservoir. An exception to the lifejacket bylaw includes paddling competitions amongst seasoned athletes.


Hall Rental Information

The Glenmore Canoe and Rowing Clubhouse is a non-smoking facility located in North Glenmore Park at the South end of Crowchild Trail. The upper hall offers a beautiful view of the lake with an outside upper deck. The banquet room can seat 100 people but is licensed for 120. There is also an outdoor deck that can be used during the summer months. The room has a small kitchen with an oven, small microwave and fridge. During the warmer months of the year (May-October) the main parking lot gets congested, there is extra parking further away in the park. There are also a few days in the summer when special events happen in the park that close the parking lot.

This location is open for bookings for weddings, parties, lunches, business meetings, and seminar and sporting groups until further notice.

2014 Summer Learn to Paddle Program

Our Youth Learn to Paddle Program offers an all around paddling experience. The program is geared to a two-week session. We feel that participants have the opportunity to experience all facets of paddling and become comfortable in the racing boats over this period of time. On water activities include paddling in recreation canoes, whitewater kayaks, sprint racing canoes and kayaks, the 14 person sprint war canoe, and 20 person dragon boat.

Basic water safety is covered along with all the basics of the equipment and strokes and skills for the various boats.

Dry land activities will make up the remainder of the day. The emphasis is on active games and events in and around the Clubhouse (soccer, capture the flag, the CCC amazing race, etc).

We realize a one week program may be more suitable for some looking for a brief introduction to paddling. Therefore, participants may sign up for a one week session. We will offer limited spaces for one week camps. One week sessions are only available for the first week of all two week sessions.

To register, choose which session you would like to start and for how many consecutive sessions you would like to participate in.

All programs take place at the Calgary Canoe Club site. The Clubhouse offers an indoor paddling tank, hall area, and sprint bay specifically for camp participants. In poor weather situations we may move activities indoors.

Pre and post care available from 8:30am - 5:30pm under the supervision of our boat bay staff, must note on your registration form.
